Risk Factors for High-Risk Pregnancy

Women in their gestation period require comprehensive care, periodic health checkups, and adequate medical guidance and attention to ensure the mother and child stay healthy. The best gynecology hospital in Bangalore suggests performing specialized monitoring at scheduled intervals to eliminate all the risk factors.
However, for the past few decades, a large chunk of pregnancies are identified as risky and need special medical care and attention. This primarily happens due to inappropriate lifestyle, poor BMI, food habits, genes, or due to stress, anxiety, or depression.
Other than this, there could be multiple other factors that can lead to high-risk pregnancies and need to be medically fixed to give birth to a healthy baby.

Common Risk Factors for a High-Risk Pregnancy

Let’s have a look at the top 8 causes leading to a high-risk pregnancy:

Preconception medical condition

A woman needs to be physically fit to be able to further the fetus and give birth to a healthy baby. With conditions like high blood pressure, diabetes, lung or kidney disease, or some kind of autoimmune disease there are chances of complications and even miscarriage.

Medical conditions during pregnancy

Gestational diabetes and preeclampsia develop during pregnancy, which can affect both the mother and the fetus, leading to a risk-prone pregnancy. Thus, it is always recommended to book an appointment at the best gynecology hospital in Bangalore to ensure that these medical conditions, if any, can be diagnosed and treated promptly.

Multiple pregnancies

In the case of twins or triplets, there could be a risk of premature labor or gestational diabetes which affects the risk levels of the pregnancy and the baby. With adequate medical attention; diet, medicine, and exercise it becomes these complications that can be managed for a smooth delivery.

Depression

Women undergo complex hormonal fluctuations during pregnancy, which can lead to depression, anxiety, and stress. These mental health challenges are also considered high-risk factors as they can lead to preterm birth, low birth weight, and cesarean section delivery.

Placenta previa

Placenta previa is a condition when the opening of the uterus(cervix) is covered by the placenta. This can lead to blood loss for the mother, contractions, and even birth injuries to the child. The symptoms usually involve bright red vaginal bleeding and must be immediately consulted with specialists at the best gynecology hospital in Ramachandrapura.

Obesity

It is the most common problem affecting women at their reproductive age. If a woman is obese, it increases the chances of low birth weight, preterm birth, miscarriage, and stillbirth. Being overweight can pave the way for multiple other complications during and after gestation period. So keeping a good BMI and a consistent check on body weight is extremely important for women during pregnancy.

Factors causing high-risk pregnancy

  • Pre-existing health conditions like thyroid, diabetes, etc,
  • Lifestyle factors include smoking,drinking, drugs.
  • Exposure to toxic elements
  • Pregnancy above 35 years or below 17 years
  • Inappropriate diet and workout regime

It is always advisable to get an appointment with a hospital in Bangalore to tackle high risk-pregnancy to understand the risk and manage the pregnancy smoothly.

Conclusion

To manage high-risk pregnancies, an advanced screening and treatment is prescribed by medical professionals. Best gynecology hospitals in Ramachandrapura are equipped with the best facilities to help women at every stage of pregnancy to ensure a smooth and safe experience.
