Diabetes During Pregnancy: Risks to the Baby

Pregnancy usually involves a series of complex emotional and physical fluctuations. So, it's understandable if you are curious and anxious about your pregnancy and the baby. Diabetes is primarily of 3 types; Type 1, Type 2, and gestational diabetes. The condition that develops during pregnancy is termed gestational diabetes.

What is gestational diabetes?

The health condition, called hyperglycemia, is when the person suffers from high levels of blood sugar. Even with no signs of pre-gestational diabetes, there are chances of developing it during the gestation period.

Insulin is the hormone that breaks glucose(sugar) from food, delivers it to our cells and is responsible for keeping body glucose at a healthy level. At around 20 to 24 weeks of pregnancy, the multitude of other hormones interfere with insulin paving the way for diabetes.

Developing GD in no way means you already have diabetes or will have it post pregnancy. In most cases it goes away after pregnancy, but could create a chance of developing Type 2. In India, 7% to 16% of pregnancies involve GD but with assistance from the best gynecologist in Ramachandrapura, the right diet and exercise you could easily take charge of gestational diabetes.

Factors leading to the development of gestational diabetes Certain factors increase the chances of developing gestational diabetes includes:
  • Family history of having diabetes
  • Women older than 25 years old
  • Being overweight and obese
  • Developing gestational diabetes since previous pregnancy
  • Weight of the baby
  • Racial and ethnicity

How does the baby get affected?

Can diabetes during pregnancy put the baby at risk? The shortest answer, Yes, it can! You need to seek help from the best gynecologist in Bangalore and follow their suggestions or it could create complications and affect the baby in some unintended ways during the pregnancy. 

The baby could develop congenital disabilities or an enlarged heart, or their could be chances of a miscarriage. 

Babies born from unattended gestational diabetes could also suffer during and after birth. There are chances of developing: 
  • Macrosomia- larger than normal size foetus
  • Hypoglycemia - Low blood sugar
  • High levels of bilirubin and thick red blood cells
  • Birth defect
  • Premature birth
  • Breathing problems
  • Changes of being obese and developing diabetes

How to diagnose gestational diabetes?

In the early stage of pregnancy, there are no symptoms to identify or diagnose until specifically tested by your Gynaecologist.

Usually, during the 24th to 28th weeks of pregnancy, the best Gynecologists in Bangalore conduct a screening test - Oral Glucose Tolerance Test (OGTT). Here the blood sugar level is tested to diagnose gestational diabetes.

How to deal with gestational diabetes?

If diagnosed with Gestational Diabetes, you would need more frequent checkups during the gestation period. The doctor could also suggest you consult a specialist.

Additionally your gestation period could involve:
  • Checking glucose level at home
  • Adhering to the prescribed medications, if any
  • Avoid junk, processed or sugary food/drinks
  • Maintain healthy balance of proteins, carbs, fiber & Fat
  • Maintain a meal schedule and have smaller meals in smaller gaps
  • Exercise as suggested by your doctor considering your age, fitness, overall health and weight.

Once you have gestational diabetes, you can’t get rid of it until the end of the gestation period. But, with the right advice from the best Pregnancy care hospitals it is always going to be a battle won without a fight.
