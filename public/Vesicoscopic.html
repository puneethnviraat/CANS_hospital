Vesicoscopic Ureteric Reimplantation

Vesicoureteric reflux is the medical term for a health condition where the patient experiences an abnormal flow of urine. The urine starts to flow from the bladder back to the ureters. Usually, urine flow is unidirectional in the human body, but when affected by this condition, it becomes bidirectional. The problem is mostly seen in children and, if left untreated, can lead to kidney damage. Now, to fix this condition, Vericoscopic Ureteric Reimplantation is done.

When is Vericoscopic Ureteric Reimplantation suggested?

There is nothing to be worried about if the medical condition is diagnosed at the right time as it can be treated and cured very easily. The treatment basically depends on the severity of the medical condition. Medications are prescribed so that the infection does not spread to the kidney. Alongside this, the kid is also monitored constantly,
However, if the condition gets too severe, then surgery, i.e., Vericoscopic Ureteric Reimplantation, is suggested. This helps to repair the defect in the valve which is present between the ureter and bladder.Â 

How is the surgery done?

During the surgery, the process in which the ureter and bladder are attached is changed. In this case, the surgery takes nearly 2-3 hours and is completely pain-free. The process by which the surgery is done depends on the condition of the child.Â Usually, the process includes the following steps:

  • The ureter is detached from the bladder; then a new tunnel is created which connects the bladder wall with the muscle.
  • Then, the ureter is placed in the new tunnel.
  • The ureter is stitched, and the bladder is closed as well.
  • If required, then the same process is repeated with both ureters. all the cuts are then stitched.
At Cans Hospitals our doctors have the expertise to perform the surgery in any of the 3-ways:
  • Open surgery: In this case, a small incision is made in the lower side of the stomach.
  • Laparoscopic surgery: The surgery is done with the help of a camera. In this case, a small surgical tool is used, and 3-4 cuts are made on the lower side of the stomach.
  • Robotic surgery: This is quite similar to laparoscopic surgery, but the only difference is that the surgery is conducted through a robot that the surgeon controls.

Usually, the child gets discharged from the hospital within 1 to 2 days.
